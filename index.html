IMRAN KHAN

The Final Hope Of Pakistan

Imran Khan is a prominent Pakistani leader, former cricketer, and philanthropist who served as the 22nd Prime Minister of Pakistan from 2018 to 2022. Before entering politics, he was an internationally celebrated cricketer and led Pakistan to victory in the 1992 Cricket World Cup. After retiring from cricket, he dedicated himself to public service by building the Shaukat Khanum Cancer Hospital, Pakistan's first cancer hospital of its kind, named after his mother. In 1996, he founded the political party Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI) with a vision of justice, anti-corruption, and public welfare. Known for his strong stance on national sovereignty and social justice, Imran Khan remains a significant figure in Pakistan's political and cultural history.

Achivements

  • 1992 Cricket World Cup Victory Led Pakistan to win its first and only ICC Cricket World Cup as captain.
  • Founder of Shaukat Khanum Cancer Hospital Built Pakistan's first cancer hospital in 1994, providing free treatment to thousands of patients.
  • Established Namal University Founded Namal Institute (now Namal University) to promote quality education in rural areas of Pakistan.
  • Founder of Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI)Entered politics in 1996 by forming PTI, which later became one of the biggest political parties in the country.
  • Became Prime Minister of Pakistan (2018) Took office as the 22nd Prime Minister, focusing on anti-corruption, justice, and economic reform.
  • Launched the Ehsaas Program Initiated one of Pakistan’s largest social welfare programs to reduce poverty and support low-income families.
  • Improved Pakistan’s Global Image Represented Pakistan on international forums with a strong voice, especially on issues like Islamophobia and Kashmir.
  • Promoted Tourism and Environmental Projects Worked on tree plantation campaigns (Billion Tree Tsunami) and eco-tourism to improve Pakistan’s environment and economy.
